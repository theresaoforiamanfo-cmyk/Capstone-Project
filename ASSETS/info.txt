These are the styling properties of a div:
1. The Box Model (Spacing & Size)
These properties define the physical space the div takes up: 

width & height: Sets the dimensions of the box (e.g., width: 300px; or height: 50%).

padding: Creates space inside the div, between the content and the border.

margin: Creates space outside the div to push other elements away.

border: Adds a line around the div. You can set its width, style (solid, dashed), and color. 


2. Layout & Positioning
These control where the div sits on the page and how it behaves: 

display: The most important layout property.

block: (Default) Takes up the full width.

flex: Turns the div into a Flexbox container to easily align items inside it.

grid: Creates a complex grid-based layout.

none: Completely removes the div from view.

position: Used for precise placement (e.g., relative, absolute, fixed, or sticky).

z-index: Controls the "stacking order" if divs overlap (higher numbers sit on top).

overflow: Decides what happens if content is too big for the div (e.g., scroll, hidden, or auto). 


3. Visuals & Background
These make the "invisible box" look like something: 

background-color: Fills the box with a solid color.

background-image: Places an image or gradient inside the div.

border-radius: Rounds the corners of the box. Use 50% to turn a square div into a circle.

box-shadow: Adds a shadow effect to give the div depth and make it look "lifted".

opacity: Sets the transparency level (0 is invisible, 1 is solid). 


4. Text Content (Inside the Div)
If you put text inside your div, these properties are used to style it: 

color: Sets the text color.

font-family & font-size: Sets the font type and size.

text-align: Aligns text to the left, right, center, or justify.

justify-content: center;: Align along the main axis (Left/Right).

align-items: center;: Align along the cross axis (Up/Down).
